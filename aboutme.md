    <h2>I like work that sits between implementation and judgment.</h2>
    <p>
      I work across data engineering, analytics, and measurement systems. The through-line is practical reliability: take messy or operationally sensitive inputs and turn them into outputs people can actually trust and use.
    </p>

    <p class="section-kicker">Background</p>
    <h2>My work sits across a few disciplines for a reason.</h2>
    <p>
      I studied Data Science and Business Economics at UC San Diego, with a minor in Urban Studies and Planning. That mix is part of why my work does not fit cleanly into a single narrow lane. I am interested in systems that sit between technical implementation and real-world trade-offs.
    </p>
    <p>
      It also explains why this site includes warehouse modeling, product measurement, transportation analysis, and policy-adjacent work without those things feeling random to me.
    </p>
  </article>

  <article class="surface-card" data-reveal>
    <p class="section-kicker">Why these topics stay</p>
    <h2>Transportation, planning, economics, and fairness still matter to how I think.</h2>
    <p>
      Transportation and urbanism were central to the earlier version of this site, and I kept that thread on purpose. Those interests shaped how I think about operations, throughput, public systems, incentives, and the downstream effects of technical decisions.
    </p>
    <p>
      They are not side notes. They are part of the reason I care about trustworthy metrics, practical fairness, and systems that hold up outside of ideal conditions.
    </p>
