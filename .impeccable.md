## Design Context

### Users
This site is primarily for recruiters and hiring managers evaluating Edwin Tse across software-adjacent engineering, data engineering, analytics engineering, product analytics, and data analyst roles. A secondary audience includes visitors who are genuinely interested in the project's subject matter and want to understand the work in more depth.

The site should help visitors quickly understand who Edwin is now, what he has built professionally, the quality of his thinking, and the range of his technical execution. It should support both rapid scanning for evaluators and deeper exploration for readers who want project detail.

### Brand Personality
The site should feel clear, credible, and modern.

It should communicate technical depth, strong execution, and sound judgment without feeling stiff or generic. The tone should balance software-adjacent engineering, analytics engineering, and product-measurement credibility while still showing that complex ideas can be explained simply and cleanly.

### Aesthetic Direction
The visual direction should be restrained, polished, and intentionally custom rather than template-driven. The reference qualities are:

- Stripe: clean, polished, structured
- Notion: minimal, calm, easy to navigate
- Linear: modern, precise, elegant

The site should avoid looking like a generic AI-generated portfolio or a stock starter template. It should feel like a product built by an experienced web developer with strong UI and UX judgment, while also carrying forward Edwin's distinct transportation, planning, economics, and fairness interests.

The interface should support both light and dark mode and follow the user's system preference automatically.

Avoid:

- Overly bright or neon colors
- Playful or cartoonish fonts
- Excessive or distracting animation
- Visual gimmicks that undermine credibility

Motion should be smooth, restrained, and purposeful. Interactivity should reinforce clarity and quality, not compete with the content.

### Design Principles
- Prioritize fast comprehension: recruiters should understand the current professional profile, strongest role fit, and project quality within seconds.
- Signal engineering credibility through precision: spacing, typography, hierarchy, copy, and interaction should feel deliberate and production-grade.
- Design for layered depth: support quick scanning first, then reward deeper reading with clearer experience narratives, project structure, and domain context.
- Keep the visual system restrained: use modern polish and interaction without relying on loud palettes or decorative excess.
- Make the experience feel custom: avoid obvious portfolio tropes, repetitive template sections, and anything that reads as generic or auto-generated.
